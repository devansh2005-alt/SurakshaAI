## 2Ô∏è‚É£ OTP Fraud

### How the scam works
Scammers trick users into sharing OTPs by pretending to be banks, KYC teams,
delivery agents, or customer support.

### Red Flags
- Asking for OTP
- Saying account will be blocked
- Urgent verification requests

### Important Rule
**Banks and government agencies NEVER ask for OTP.**

### What to Do
- Never share OTP
- Disconnect the call/message
- Inform your bank immediately if OTP is shared

## 3Ô∏è‚É£ APK / Fake App Scam (Very Common in Rural Areas)

### How the scam works
Victims are asked to download APK files from WhatsApp or links claiming to be
government apps (e.g., kisan.apk, pm.apk).

Once installed, scammers gain control or steal data.

### Red Flags
- APK file sent on WhatsApp
- ‚ÄúGovernment app‚Äù not available on Play Store
- Instructions to enable ‚ÄúInstall from unknown sources‚Äù

### Golden Rule
**Government apps are ONLY available on the Google Play Store.**

### What to Do
- Do NOT install APKs
- Delete the file immediately
- Scan phone if already installed
- Call 1930 if fraud occurs

## 5Ô∏è‚É£ Video Call & Blackmail Scam

### How the scam works
Scammers initiate video calls and record private moments, then blackmail victims
for money.

### Red Flags
- Unknown video calls
- Requests for private actions
- Threats to leak videos

### What to Do Immediately
- Disconnect the call
- Block the contact
- Do NOT pay
- Report on cybercrime.gov.in

---

## 6Ô∏è‚É£ Fraud Links & Phishing

### How the scam works
Victims receive fake links for KYC update, prizes, refunds, or UPI issues.

### Red Flags
- Shortened links
- Messages urging immediate action
- Asking to log in or approve payments

### What to Do
- Never click unknown links
- Verify through official apps or websites
- Do not approve unknown UPI requests

## Final Safety Advice

- Stay calm during any urgent call or message
- Fraud works by creating fear and panic
- Pause, verify, and seek help
